How This Salary Predictor Works

This tool uses a Logistic Regression machine learning model trained on 250,000 job salary records to predict annual salaries for professionals. It takes your professional information and outputs a salary estimate with a confidence level.

The Model: Logistic Regression

We selected Logistic Regression as our prediction model because it achieved the highest accuracy among tested algorithms (95.4% F1-Score). It is a statistical method that:

  • Uses the Sigmoid function to transform input values into probability predictions
  • Classifies you as a "High Earner" or "Standard Earner" based on your profile
  • Is interpretable and provides clear feature importance rankings
  • Generalizes well to unseen data
The Sigmoid Function
The model uses the formula: P(High Earner) = 1 / (1 + e^(-z))
This converts a weighted sum of your features into a probability between 0 and 1. If probability > 0.5, you are predicted to be a high earner.

How Your Salary Gets Predicted

1
Collect Your Information

Job title, experience, education, skills, industry, company size, location, and certifications are gathered.

2
Encode Categorical Features

Text fields are converted into numerical representations that the model understands.

3
Normalize Numerical Features

Experience years, skills count, and certifications are standardized to the same scale for fair comparison.

4
Calculate Prediction

The model applies learned weights to all 42 encoded features and outputs a probability of being a high earner.

5
Estimate Salary Range

Based on the prediction and industry benchmarks, the tool calculates your estimated annual salary.

Understanding Your Prediction

Salary Calculation

Base Salary + (Experience Years x $2,000) + (Skills Count x $1,000), capped to realistic ranges per industry and location

Confidence Levels

  • 90-100%: Very high confidence - your profile matches well-established patterns
  • 70-89%: Good confidence - prediction is reliable for your circumstances
  • 50-69%: Moderate confidence - your profile is less common, estimate may vary
  • Below 50%: Low confidence - consider other factors in your negotiation

Important Notes

This is an Estimate, Not a Guarantee

Actual salaries vary based on negotiation skills, economic conditions, company performance, and individual circumstances.
